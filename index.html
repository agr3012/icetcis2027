About the Conference

Why ICETCIS 2027

ICETCIS 2027 brings together researchers, academicians, and industry practitioners to share original work at the intersection of artificial intelligence, cloud systems, and cyber defence. Hosted by SITAICS at Rashtriya Raksha University, the conference pairs rigorous double-blind peer review with a hybrid format open to participants from India and abroad — a focused venue for work that sits squarely at the boundary of intelligent systems and security.

Rigorous Peer Review

Every submission undergoes double-blind review by a minimum of three independent reviewers, coordinated through Microsoft CMT, with plagiarism screening prior to acceptance.

International Participation

Technical Programme Committees drawn from institutions across India and abroad, spanning academia, government research bodies, and global industry R&D.

Hybrid Access

Onsite sessions at the Rashtriya Raksha University campus, Gandhinagar, with parallel online participation so authors and delegates worldwide can present and attend.

Technical Scope

Three Tracks, One Conference

ICETCIS 2027 invites original, unpublished research across the following tracks.

Track 01

Cyber Security and Digital Forensics

Malware analysis, memory & mobile forensics, incident response, network intrusion detection, VAPT, post-quantum cryptography, IoT/OT security, cyber law & digital evidence.

Track 02

Intelligent Systems and Applications

Machine learning, deep learning, NLP & large language models, computer vision, federated & privacy-preserving AI, explainable AI, autonomous systems, AI in healthcare & smart cities.

Track 03

Emerging Computing Technologies

Cloud & edge computing, blockchain, quantum computing, IoT & cyber-physical systems, DevSecOps, big data analytics, 5G/6G networks, green computing.

Publication

Proceedings & Indexing

Proposed Publication Partner: Springer — Communications in Computer and Information Science (CCIS) series, subject to formal proposal approval. The proceedings proposal is being submitted through the standard CCIS review process; the conference will use the Springer branding and templates only after written confirmation is received from the publisher.

All accepted papers will be screened for plagiarism (similarity threshold below 15%, excluding references and quotations) prior to inclusion in the camera-ready package. Submission and review will be managed through Microsoft CMT.
